# Cover Letter

Dear Editor,

We submit our manuscript entitled "Machine Learning Classification of Breast Cancer Using Fine-Needle Aspiration Cytology Features: A Diagnostic Accuracy Study" for consideration as an original research article in *Radiology: Artificial Intelligence*.

This study provides a standardized STARD-compliant comparison of three machine learning classifiers (logistic regression, random forest, and support vector machine) for FNA-based breast cancer classification using 30 nuclear morphometric features. All three classifiers achieved AUC values exceeding 0.99 on an independent test set, with SVM demonstrating the highest accuracy (0.974) and perfect specificity. To our knowledge, this represents one of the first STARD-compliant analyses with complete confidence intervals and pairwise statistical testing on this benchmark dataset.

This manuscript aligns with *Radiology: Artificial Intelligence*'s focus on AI applications in medical imaging and diagnostic pathology. The study design follows STARD 2015 reporting standards and includes comprehensive performance metrics.

This is a single-institution retrospective diagnostic accuracy study using 569 FNA cytology samples with stratified train-test splitting.

This manuscript has not been published elsewhere and is not under consideration at another journal. All authors have read and approved the manuscript. The authors declare no conflicts of interest. No funding was received for this work.

All authors have reviewed and take responsibility for the final content. The AI tool was not listed as an author and did not contribute to study conception, design, or data interpretation.
