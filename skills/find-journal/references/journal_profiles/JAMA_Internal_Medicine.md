## Scope
JAMA Internal Medicine publishes original research, reviews, and commentary focused on internal medicine and its subspecialties. It emphasizes studies with direct implications for clinical practice, health policy, and medical decision-making, particularly those addressing overdiagnosis, overtreatment, patient-centered outcomes, and healthcare value.

## Special Notes
JAMA Internal Medicine is among the highest-impact subspecialty journals (IF ~39) and frequently publishes practice-changing studies. Known for its "Less Is More" series on overdiagnosis and overtreatment. Follows JAMA Network structured abstract format (7-heading) and Key Points box. Competitive acceptance rate (~8-10%). Transferred manuscripts from JAMA are commonly considered. AI policy: language editing only, Methods + cover letter disclosure required per JAMA Network policy.
