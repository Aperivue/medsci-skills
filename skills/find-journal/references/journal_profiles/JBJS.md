## Scope
JBJS publishes original clinical research, systematic reviews, and current concepts in orthopaedic surgery. It covers musculoskeletal trauma, joint replacement, sports medicine, spine surgery, pediatric orthopaedics, and surgical outcomes research. The journal values Level I-II evidence, large cohort studies, and validated patient-reported outcomes.

## Special Notes
JBJS is the oldest and highest-impact orthopaedic journal. It assigns a Level of Evidence (I-V) to all clinical studies, and Level I-II evidence is strongly preferred. The journal emphasizes functional outcomes with minimum 2-year follow-up for arthroplasty studies. AI/ML studies are considered when directly applicable to orthopaedic clinical decision-making or imaging interpretation. JBJS has a companion journal (JBJS Reviews) for narrative reviews.
