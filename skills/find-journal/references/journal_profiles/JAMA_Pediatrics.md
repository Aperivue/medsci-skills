## Scope
JAMA Pediatrics publishes original research, reviews, and clinical investigations focused on diseases and health conditions in infants, children, and adolescents. It emphasizes studies with broad clinical relevance, rigorous methodology, and potential to influence pediatric practice, policy, or public health.

## Special Notes
JAMA Pediatrics is the highest-impact pediatric specialty journal (IF ~26) and part of the JAMA Network. Manuscripts can be transferred within the JAMA Network system. It strongly favors large population-based studies, randomized trials, and systematic reviews that inform pediatric guidelines or public health policy. Structured abstracts and adherence to reporting guidelines (CONSORT, STROBE, PRISMA) are mandatory.
