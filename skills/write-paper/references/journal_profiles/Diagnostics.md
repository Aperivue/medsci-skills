# Journal Profile: Diagnostics (MDPI)

## Journal Identity

- **Full name**: Diagnostics
- **Abbreviation**: Diagnostics (Basel)
- **Publisher**: MDPI (Multidisciplinary Digital Publishing Institute)
- **ISSN**: 2075-4418
- **Frequency**: Continuous (semimonthly issues)
- **Impact Factor**: ~3.0 (JCR 2023)
- **Open Access**: Full open access (gold OA)
- **APC**: ~2,600 CHF (subject to change; verify at journal site)
- **Acceptance rate**: ~40-50%
- **Peer review**: Single-blind; rapid review (median first decision ~18 days)

## Manuscript Types and Word Limits

| Type | Body Word Limit | Abstract | References | Figures/Tables |
|------|----------------|----------|------------|----------------|
| Original Article | No strict limit (typically 3000-5000) | 200 words (single paragraph) | No strict limit | No strict limit |
| Review | No strict limit | 200 words | No strict limit | No strict limit |
| Systematic Review | No strict limit | 200 words | No strict limit | No strict limit |
| Brief Report | 2000 words | 200 words | 20 | 4 |
| Case Report | 3000 words | 200 words | 20 | 6 |
| Communication | 2000 words | 200 words | 20 | 2 |

MDPI journals generally have flexible word limits. Quality and completeness are prioritized.

---

## Abstract Requirements

**Unstructured abstract, 200 words maximum:**

Single paragraph. Should cover: background, objective, methods, key results, conclusion. No references in abstract. No abbreviations in first use.

---

## Required Journal-Specific Elements

### MDPI Formatting

- **LaTeX or Word**: MDPI provides templates for both
- **Section numbering**: All sections numbered (1. Introduction, 2. Materials and Methods, etc.)
- **Abbreviations section**: Required after Conclusions — alphabetical list of all abbreviations used
- **Supplementary Materials**: Referenced as "Figure S1, Table S1" etc.
- **Author Contributions**: CRediT taxonomy mandatory
- **Funding**: Explicit funding statement required
- **Data Availability Statement**: Mandatory (template provided)
- **Conflicts of Interest**: Mandatory declaration
- **Informed Consent Statement**: Required for studies involving humans
- **Ethics Statement**: Required with IRB name and approval number

---

## Required Sections (Original Article)

1. **Introduction** — background, gap, objective (numbered section 1)
2. **Materials and Methods** (numbered section 2)
   - Study Design
   - Participants/Data
   - Procedures/Imaging Protocol
   - Statistical Analysis
   - Ethics
3. **Results** (numbered section 3)
4. **Discussion** (numbered section 4)
5. **Conclusions** (numbered section 5, separate from Discussion)
6. **Abbreviations** — alphabetical table
7. **Supplementary Materials** (if any)
8. **Author Contributions**
9. **Funding**
10. **Data Availability Statement**
11. **Conflicts of Interest**
12. **Ethics Statements** (Institutional Review Board, Informed Consent)

---

## Statistical Reporting

- Report p-values with adequate precision (e.g., p = 0.032).
- 95% CI for primary outcomes recommended.
- For diagnostic accuracy: sensitivity, specificity, AUC with 95% CI.
- Clearly describe all statistical tests used.
- Sample size justification recommended but not as strictly enforced as top-tier journals.
- Statistical software and version must be identified.

---

## Figures

- **No strict figure limit** for full articles
- **Resolution**: 300 DPI minimum (1000 DPI for line art)
- **Format**: TIFF, PNG, JPEG (high quality), EPS, PDF
- **Color**: Free (full OA)
- **Size**: Figures should be legible at single-column width (85 mm) or full page width (180 mm)
- **MDPI style**: Figures numbered sequentially; captions below; multi-panel with (a), (b), (c) labels

---

## Common Rejection Reasons

1. **Ethical issues** — missing IRB, informed consent, or registration
2. **Plagiarism/overlap** — MDPI runs iThenticate check; high similarity flagged
3. **Very low methodological quality** — despite higher acceptance rate, basic standards enforced
4. **Scope mismatch** — Diagnostics is broad but focused on diagnostic methods
5. **Formatting non-compliance** — MDPI has specific template requirements

## Special Issues

Diagnostics frequently publishes **Special Issues** on focused topics. Submitting to an open Special Issue can:
- Increase acceptance likelihood (dedicated editors recruiting papers)
- Provide themed context for niche studies
- Result in faster review (Special Issue editors are proactive)

Check open Special Issues at the journal homepage before submission.

---

## Positioning

Diagnostics is appropriate when:
- Diagnostic accuracy study with adequate methodology but moderate sample size
- AI/ML applied to diagnostic imaging or laboratory diagnostics
- Systematic review or meta-analysis on diagnostic topics
- Pilot or proof-of-concept study that needs open access visibility
- Study previously rejected from higher-tier specialty journal but methodologically sound

Not appropriate for: pure therapeutic intervention studies, studies with no diagnostic component, papers requiring prestige placement.
