# Journal Profile: Clinical Journal of the American Society of Nephrology (CJASN)

## Journal Identity

- **Full name**: Clinical Journal of the American Society of Nephrology
- **Abbreviation**: Clin J Am Soc Nephrol
- **Publisher**: Wolters Kluwer on behalf of the American Society of Nephrology (ASN)
- **ISSN**: 1555-9041 (print), 1555-905X (online)
- **Frequency**: Monthly
- **Impact Factor**: ~9.6 (JCR 2023)
- **Open Access**: Hybrid
- **Acceptance rate**: ~12-15%
- **Peer review**: Single-blind, 2-3 reviewers

## Manuscript Types and Word Limits

| Type | Body Word Limit | Abstract | References | Figures/Tables |
|------|----------------|----------|------------|----------------|
| Original Article | 3000 words | 250 words | 50 | 4 figs & 4 tables (8 max) |
| Review | 4000 words | 250 words | 100 | 6 (combined) |
| Expedited Report | 2000 words | 150 words | 50 | 3 (combined) |
| Case Report | Not accepted | - | - | - |

*Note: CJASN does not publish case reports. Body word limit excludes Significance Statement, Methods, references, figure legends, and tables.*

## Abstract Requirements

**Structured abstract, 250 words maximum:**
Headings must be exactly:
- **Background:** [Clinical problem, gaps in knowledge]
- **Methods:** [Design, setting, study population, intervention/exposure, outcomes, statistical analysis]
- **Results:** [Main findings with absolute and relative effects, 95% CIs, exact p-values]
- **Conclusions:** [Summary of clinical impact and implications]

**Significance Statement (Mandatory):**
- A separate section of **120 words or less** positioned before the abstract.
- Explains the significance of the research for clinical practice and patient care in accessible language.

---

## Required Sections (Original Article)

1. **Title Page**
2. **Significance Statement** (max 120 words)
3. **Structured Abstract** (max 250 words)
4. **Introduction** (brief, ending with study rationale and objectives)
5. **Methods** (positioned after Introduction; must details IRB, registry numbers, and cohort details)
6. **Results** (ordered by importance; include descriptive Table 1, primary, and secondary outcomes)
7. **Discussion** (comparison with literature, clinical implications, study-level limitations, final conclusion)
8. **Acknowledgments**
9. **Disclosures**
10. **Funding**
11. **References**

---

## Statistical Reporting

- Report exact p-values; thresholds (P < 0.05) are discouraged unless P < 0.001.
- Report 95% Confidence Intervals for all relative risks, hazard ratios, and odds ratios.
- Emphasize clinical significance over statistical significance; report absolute risk reductions.
- **Confounding in Observational Studies:** As CJASN is heavily clinical and epidemiological, observational studies must utilize robust confounding adjustment methods (e.g., propensity score matching, inverse probability weighting, or multivariable regression) with clear diagnostics (e.g., standardized mean differences).
- **Competing Risks:** Address competing risks of death or kidney transplantation when analyzing long-term chronic kidney disease cohorts.

## Figures

- **Maximum**: 4 figures and 4 tables (maximum 8 display items combined).

- **Color**: Free in online publication.

## Common Rejection Reasons

1. **Inadequate Confounder Adjustment:** For observational research, failing to properly control for bias and confounding.
2. **Single-Center Retrospective Cohort:** Lacking generalizability to broader patient populations.
3. **Lack of Novelty:** Study confirms well-established clinical associations without novel insights or practice-changing angles.
4. **Case Reports Submitted:** CJASN explicitly does not accept case reports.
5. **Absence of Patient-Centered Outcomes:** Focuses on surrogate biomarkers without established clinical significance, without clinical endpoints (e.g., ESRD, doubling of creatinine, or mortality).

## Positioning

CJASN is the premier clinical journal of the ASN. It is best suited for clinical trials, well-designed observational studies, clinical epidemiology, and health services research in nephrology.
