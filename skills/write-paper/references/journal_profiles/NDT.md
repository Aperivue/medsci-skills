# Journal Profile: Nephrology Dialysis Transplantation (NDT)

## Journal Identity

- **Full name**: Nephrology Dialysis Transplantation
- **Abbreviation**: Nephrol Dial Transplant
- **Publisher**: Oxford University Press on behalf of the European Renal Association (ERA)
- **ISSN**: 0931-0509 (print), 1460-2385 (online)
- **Frequency**: Monthly
- **Impact Factor**: ~6.1 (JCR 2023)
- **Open Access**: Hybrid
- **Acceptance rate**: ~15-18%
- **Peer review**: Single-blind, 2 reviewers

## Manuscript Types and Word Limits

| Type | Body Word Limit | Abstract | References | Figures/Tables |
|------|----------------|----------|------------|----------------|
| Original Article | 3500 words | 300 words | 50 | 6 (combined) |
| Research Letter | 1000 words | None | 15 | 2 (combined) |
| Editorial | 1500 words | None | 20 | 1 (combined) |
| NDT Review | 4000 words | 250 words | 100 | 4 (combined) |

*Note: Body word limit includes the abstract, but excludes references, tables, figures, and legends. Submissions must be in Word format.*

## Abstract Requirements

**Structured abstract, 300 words maximum:**
Headings must be exactly:
- **Background:** [Clinical gap, hypothesis, and objective]
- **Methods:** [Study design, population, main variables, analyses]
- **Results:** [Primary results with hazard/odds ratios, 95% CIs, and exact p-values]
- **Conclusions:** [Summary and clinical interpretation]

**Keywords:** Maximum of 5 keywords are required immediately below the abstract.

---

## Required Sections (Original Article)

1. **Title Page**
2. **Structured Abstract** (max 300 words)
3. **Introduction** (short, ending with explicit hypothesis/aim)
4. **Methods** (ethics approvals, details of methods, and statistics)
5. **Results** (presentation of the data in text and display items)
6. **Discussion** (critical comparison with literature, strengths and limitations, final conclusion)
7. **Conflict of Interest Statement**
8. **Funding Statement**
9. **References**

---

## Statistical Reporting

- Report exact p-values (P = 0.015), not thresholds.
- Standard 95% Confidence Intervals (95% CI) and clinical effect sizes must be provided.
- Statistical software and version must be explicitly identified.
- In chronic outcome analyses, competing risk analysis (competing risk of death before dialysis or transplant) is highly recommended and expected by reviewers.

---

## Figures

- **Maximum**: 6 figures/tables combined for Original Articles.
- **Resolution**: 300 DPI for half-tones, 1200 DPI for line drawings.
- **Format**: Preferred formats are high-resolution TIF or EPS.
- **Color**: Color figures are free in the electronic edition; print color may be subject to page charges.

---

## Common Rejection Reasons

1. **Low Generalizability:** Single-center study showing regional findings with no broader international relevance.
2. **Incremental Finding:** Study replicates well-known findings with no novel clinical or scientific breakthrough.
3. **Underpowered Study:** Small sample sizes that do not allow robust conclusions.
4. **Survival Censoring Issues:** Censoring patients who die instead of treating death as a competing event.
5. **Exceeding Word Limits:** Submitting papers over 3,500 words (including abstract).

---

## Cover Letter

Should include:
- A brief statement on the novelty and importance of the work.
- Suggestion of 3-5 independent potential reviewers with their email addresses.
- Statements confirming author approval, originality of the work, and no duplicate submission.
