# Journal Profile: Circulation

## Journal Identity

- **Full name**: Circulation
- **Abbreviation**: Circulation
- **Publisher**: American Heart Association (AHA) / Wolters Kluwer
- **ISSN**: 0009-7322 (print), 1524-4539 (online)
- **Frequency**: Weekly (52 issues/year)
- **Impact Factor**: ~38 (JCR 2023), ranked 1st among cardiology journals
- **Open Access**: Hybrid (optional OA with APC ~$4,000)
- **Acceptance rate**: ~10% for Original Research Articles
- **Peer review**: Single-blind; dedicated statistical reviewer for all accepted manuscripts

## Abstract Requirements

**Structured abstract, 250 words maximum:**

```
Background: [Context and knowledge gap — why this study is needed]
Methods: [Study design, setting, population, key methods, outcomes]
Results: [Primary results with effect sizes, 95% CIs, and P values;
    number of participants analyzed]
Conclusions: [Main conclusion directly answering the study objective;
    clinical implications]
```

**Critical**: Circulation uses a 4-heading structured abstract (Background, Methods, Results, Conclusions). Unlike JAMA, there is no "Importance" or "Objective" heading. The Background should be concise (2-3 sentences max). Results should lead with the primary endpoint.

## Required Journal-Specific Elements

### 1. Clinical Perspective Box

Required for all Original Research Articles. Three components:

- **What Is New?** — 2-3 bullet points stating what this study adds to existing knowledge.
- **What Are the Clinical Implications?** — 2-3 bullet points on how findings affect clinical practice.

Example:
```
Clinical Perspective

What Is New?
• AI-based coronary calcium scoring on non-gated chest CT
  accurately predicts major adverse cardiovascular events.
• Automated scoring showed equivalent prognostic value to
  ECG-gated dedicated calcium scoring.

What Are the Clinical Implications?
• Opportunistic screening from routine chest CTs may identify
  at-risk patients without additional imaging.
• Integration into radiology workflows could enable population-
  level cardiovascular risk stratification.
```

### 2. Nonstandard Abbreviations and Acronyms

A list of all nonstandard abbreviations used in the manuscript, placed before the introduction.

### 3. Article Information

- Author affiliations with department, institution, city, country
- Corresponding author full contact details
- Sources of funding (complete disclosure required)
- Disclosures: each author's conflicts listed individually
- Data availability statement
- Supplemental material reference

## Required Sections (Original Research Article)

1. **Introduction** — 3-4 paragraphs; state the knowledge gap and end with explicit study objective
2. **Methods**
   - Ethics: IRB/ethics committee approval with institution name
   - Study Design and Population: inclusion/exclusion criteria, enrollment dates
   - Procedures/Interventions (if applicable)
   - Outcomes: primary and secondary endpoints clearly defined
   - Statistical Analysis: software, tests, sample size calculation, handling of missing data, multiplicity correction
3. **Results** — begin with study population description and flow diagram; present primary endpoint first
4. **Discussion** — 4-6 paragraphs; contextualize findings, address limitations in dedicated paragraph, end with future directions
5. **Conclusions** — brief (2-3 sentences), avoid overclaiming

## Statistical Reporting

Circulation maintains rigorous statistical standards with dedicated biostatistician review:
- Report exact P values to 3 decimal places (e.g., P = 0.032); use P < 0.001 below that threshold.
- 95% CI required for all primary effect estimates.
- Effect sizes with appropriate metrics (HR, OR, absolute risk difference, mean difference).
- For diagnostic/prognostic studies: AUC with 95% CI, calibration assessment, reclassification indices (NRI, IDI).
- Sample size justification required in Methods (power calculation with effect size assumptions).
- Primary outcome must be pre-specified; multiplicity adjustment required for multiple comparisons.
- Sensitivity analyses encouraged (e.g., propensity matching, inverse probability weighting for observational studies).
- Report both relative and absolute measures of effect when applicable.
- Time-to-event analyses: Kaplan-Meier curves with numbers at risk, log-rank test, Cox regression with proportional hazards assumption checked.
- Statistical software and version must be stated.

## Figures

- **Maximum 7 figures/tables combined** for Original Research
- **Resolution**: 300 DPI minimum (600 DPI for line art)
- **Format**: EPS, TIFF, or PDF; PowerPoint accepted but discouraged
- **Color**: Free in print and online
- **Supplementary material**: Unlimited online-only supplement (Data Supplement)
- **Figure panels**: Label A, B, C with self-explanatory legends
- **Central Illustration**: Strongly encouraged — a single graphical summary figure conveying the main message
- **CONSORT/STROBE flow**: Required where applicable, counts as one figure

## Common Rejection Reasons

1. **Incremental advance over existing literature** — Circulation expects novel, practice-changing findings in cardiology
2. **Insufficient sample size or single-center design** — strongly prefers large multicenter or registry-based studies
3. **Missing Clinical Perspective box** — required at submission for Original Research
4. **Lack of clinical endpoint** — technical studies (imaging, biomarker) without hard outcomes (MACE, mortality) are difficult to publish
5. **Statistical deficiencies** — underpowered primary analysis, no multiplicity correction, missing sensitivity analyses
6. **Narrow subspecialty scope** — findings limited to one cardiac subspecialty may be redirected to Circulation companion journals
7. **AI/imaging without clinical validation** — technical performance metrics alone insufficient; must show clinical workflow or outcome impact

## Cover Letter

Must include:
- Brief statement of novelty and clinical significance (2-3 sentences)
- Statement that manuscript is not under consideration elsewhere
- Confirmation all authors approved the final version and met ICMJE criteria
- Disclosure of related manuscripts submitted or in press
- Statement of potential overlap with companion journal submissions (if dual submission within Circulation family)
- Suggested reviewers (3-5 recommended)

## AI Writing Disclosure Policy
- **Requirement level:** Required
- **Permitted scope:** Follows AHA policy — AI/LLM tools may be used for language editing and manuscript preparation; AI cannot be listed as an author; authors are responsible for all content including AI-assisted text
- **Disclosure location:** Methods section — describe the AI tool name, version, and how it was used; per ICMJE requirements
- **AI-generated images:** Must be disclosed; fabrication/falsification policies apply to all images regardless of generation method

## Positioning

| Criterion | Circulation | European Heart Journal | JACC |
|-----------|-------------|----------------------|------|
| **Society** | AHA | ESC | ACC |
| **Impact Factor** | ~38 | ~35 | ~22 |
| **Geographic strength** | North America, global | Europe, global | North America |
| **Guidelines** | AHA/ACC joint guidelines | ESC Guidelines | ACC/AHA joint guidelines |
| **Statistical rigor** | Very high (dedicated reviewer) | High | High |
| **AI/imaging studies** | Requires clinical endpoints | Open to imaging innovation | JACC:CV Imaging preferred |
| **Transfer system** | 6 companion journals | ESC journal family | 5 JACC family journals |
| **Abstract style** | 4-heading structured | 4-heading structured | Unstructured (250 words) |
| **Central Illustration** | Strongly encouraged | Encouraged | Encouraged |
| **Dual submission** | Within Circulation family (declare) | No | Within JACC family (declare) |

**Choose Circulation when:**
- Large RCT, registry study, or epidemiological cohort with cardiovascular hard endpoints (MACE, mortality)
- AHA-relevant topic (prevention, guidelines, stroke, vascular biology)
- AI/imaging study validated against clinical outcomes in large populations
- Study with broad cardiology relevance, not limited to one subspecialty

**Choose companion journals instead:**
- Circulation: Cardiovascular Imaging — cardiac imaging methods, AI imaging without hard endpoints
- Circulation: Arrhythmia and Electrophysiology — EP-specific studies
- Circulation: Heart Failure — HF-specific studies
- Circulation: Cardiovascular Interventions — procedural outcomes
