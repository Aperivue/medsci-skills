# Journal Profile: Journal of Clinical Oncology

## Journal Identity

- **Full name**: Journal of Clinical Oncology
- **Abbreviation**: J Clin Oncol (JCO)
- **Publisher**: American Society of Clinical Oncology (ASCO) / Wolters Kluwer
- **ISSN**: 0732-183X (print), 1527-7755 (online)
- **Frequency**: 36 issues/year (3 per month)
- **Impact Factor**: ~42 (JCR 2023), ranked 1st among oncology journals by citation volume
- **Open Access**: Hybrid (optional OA with APC ~$5,000)
- **Acceptance rate**: ~8% for Original Reports
- **Peer review**: Single-blind; mandatory dedicated biostatistician review for all manuscripts

## Abstract Requirements

**Structured abstract, 250 words maximum:**

```
Purpose: [Clear statement of the study objective]
Methods: [Study design, setting, eligibility criteria, interventions/exposures,
    primary and key secondary endpoints, statistical methods]
Results: [Number of patients, key demographic summary, primary endpoint
    results with effect sizes, 95% CIs, and P values]
Conclusion: [Main conclusion — 1-2 sentences directly answering the Purpose;
    clinical implications]
```

**Critical**: JCO uses a 4-heading structured abstract (Purpose, Methods, Results, Conclusion — note singular "Conclusion"). The Purpose heading must be a concise statement of the research question. The Conclusion must not overclaim beyond what the data support.

## Required Journal-Specific Elements

### 1. Context Box (Rapid Communications) / Visual Abstract (encouraged)

For Rapid Communications, a structured Context box is required:

- **Key Objective**: One sentence
- **Knowledge Generated**: Key finding (quantitative)
- **Relevance (Salk et al)**: How does this change clinical practice or future research?

For Original Reports, a Visual Abstract (graphical summary) is strongly encouraged for social media dissemination.

### 2. Data Sharing Statement

Mandatory. Must specify:
- Whether individual-level data will be shared
- Type of data available (deidentified, aggregate)
- Where/how to access (repository, upon request)
- Timeframe of availability

### 3. Author Information

- Author affiliations with department, institution, city, country
- Corresponding author email
- Equal contribution statements (if applicable)
- ORCID (strongly encouraged for all authors)
- Author contributions (CRediT taxonomy)
- Conflict of interest disclosures (ASCO COI form required from all authors)
- Funding statement with grant numbers

## Required Sections (Original Report)

1. **Introduction** — brief (2-3 paragraphs); state the rationale and end with explicit study objective; avoid extensive literature review
2. **Methods**
   - Ethics: IRB/ethics approval with institution name, informed consent statement
   - Study Design and Patients: eligibility criteria, enrollment period, study setting
   - Treatment/Intervention (if applicable): dosing, schedule, modifications
   - Assessments/Endpoints: primary and secondary endpoints defined per standard criteria (RECIST, WHO, etc.)
   - Statistical Analysis: pre-specified analysis plan, software, sample size justification, interim analyses, multiplicity correction, missing data handling
3. **Results** — begin with patient disposition (CONSORT diagram for trials); baseline table; present primary endpoint first, then secondary/exploratory
4. **Discussion** — 4-5 paragraphs; contextualize findings with existing evidence, address limitations (dedicated paragraph), discuss generalizability, conclude with clinical implications
5. **Conclusion** — 1-2 sentences only (do not repeat Discussion content)

## Statistical Reporting

JCO has the most rigorous statistical review process among oncology journals:
- Report exact P values (e.g., P = .03 or P = .001); use P < .001 threshold; two-sided tests assumed unless stated otherwise.
- 95% CI required for all primary and key secondary outcomes — considered more informative than P values alone.
- Hazard ratios (Cox regression) for time-to-event; odds ratios or risk ratios for binary outcomes.
- Kaplan-Meier survival curves with numbers at risk at each time point.
- Median follow-up with method stated (reverse Kaplan-Meier preferred).
- Sample size calculation required: state alpha, power, expected effect size, accrual assumptions.
- Interim analysis: describe stopping rules (O'Brien-Fleming, Lan-DeMets) and alpha spending function.
- Multiple comparisons: hierarchical testing or Bonferroni/Holm correction; uncontrolled exploratory analyses must be explicitly labeled.
- Subgroup analyses: pre-specified in protocol; present as forest plot with interaction P values.
- Missing data: describe mechanism (MCAR/MAR/MNAR), imputation method if used, sensitivity analysis.
- Propensity score methods: describe variables, balance assessment (standardized differences), type (matching/weighting/stratification).
- Statistical software and version required in Methods.

## Figures

- **Maximum 6 figures/tables combined** for Original Reports
- **Resolution**: 300 DPI minimum (600 DPI for line art)
- **Format**: TIFF, EPS, or high-resolution PDF
- **Color**: Free in online and print
- **Supplementary material**: Unlimited online-only appendix (Data Supplement)
- **Figure panels**: Label A, B, C; legends must be comprehensive and self-explanatory
- **Kaplan-Meier curves**: Must include numbers at risk below x-axis, median survival lines
- **Forest plots**: Standard format with effect estimate, CI, weight, and overall diamond
- **CONSORT flow diagram**: Required for all RCTs, counts as one figure
- **Waterfall/swimmer plots**: Encouraged for early-phase and immunotherapy trials

## Common Rejection Reasons

1. **Insufficient clinical novelty** — JCO expects practice-changing findings; confirmatory studies of established treatments difficult to publish
2. **Underpowered study or non-informative trial** — study must meet its primary endpoint or provide definitive negative result; underpowered negative trials rarely accepted
3. **Statistical deficiencies** — dedicated biostatistician review catches: missing multiplicity correction, post-hoc endpoint changes, inadequate survival analysis
4. **Single-center or single-arm without context** — phase II single-arm acceptable only with validated historical controls or exceptionally novel interventions
5. **Overclaiming in conclusion** — strict adherence to what data support; causal language in observational studies flagged immediately
6. **AI/biomarker without clinical validation** — discovery/development-phase studies without independent validation cohort should go to JCO Precision Oncology or JCO Clinical Cancer Informatics
7. **Narrow tumor type without broader implications** — rare tumors acceptable but must discuss generalizable principles
8. **Incomplete follow-up** — immature survival data without justification for early reporting

## Cover Letter

Must include:
- Statement of novelty and why JCO specifically (1-2 sentences on clinical significance)
- Statement that manuscript is not under consideration elsewhere
- Confirmation all authors have read and approved the manuscript
- ASCO conflict of interest disclosure reference
- Declaration of prior presentation (conference abstract, ASCO meeting)
- Suggested reviewers (recommended; provide expertise areas)
- Related manuscripts submitted or in press (required disclosure)

## Positioning

| Criterion | JCO | Lancet Oncology | JAMA Oncology |
|-----------|-----|----------------|---------------|
| **Society** | ASCO | Lancet Group | AMA (JAMA Network) |
| **Impact Factor** | ~42 | ~36 | ~28 |
| **Statistical rigor** | Extremely high (mandatory biostatistician) | High | Very high (JAMA process) |
| **Geographic strength** | North America, global | International, European strength | North America |
| **Phase III trials** | Primary destination | Strong, esp. international | Selective |
| **AI/informatics** | JCO CCI companion | Lancet Digital Health redirect | JAMA Network Open redirect |
| **Guidelines** | ASCO Guidelines/Standards | None (policy-focused) | None |
| **Transfer system** | JCO CCI, Precision Oncology, Oncology Practice | Lancet family (eClinicalMedicine, Lancet DH) | JAMA Network journals |
| **Abstract style** | 4-heading (Purpose/Methods/Results/Conclusion) | Unstructured + Research in Context | 7-heading JAMA style |
| **Visual Abstract** | Encouraged | Required (Research in Context) | Key Points box required |
| **Data sharing** | Mandatory statement | Required | Mandatory statement |

**Choose JCO when:**
- Phase III RCT or large multi-institutional clinical trial with hard endpoints (OS, PFS, DFS)
- ASCO-relevant topic (therapeutic advances, supportive care, cancer care delivery)
- Practice-changing finding that will inform ASCO guidelines
- AI/biomarker study with external validation and clear clinical decision impact
- Definitive negative trial of an established regimen

**Choose companion journals instead:**
- JCO Precision Oncology — genomic/molecular studies, biomarker development without clinical validation
- JCO Clinical Cancer Informatics — AI methodology, computational tools, health informatics
- JCO Oncology Practice — care delivery, quality improvement, health services research
- JCO Global Oncology — studies focused on low/middle-income countries
