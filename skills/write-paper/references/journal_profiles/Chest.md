# Journal Profile: CHEST

## Journal Identity

- **Full name**: CHEST (Journal of the American College of Chest Physicians)
- **Abbreviation**: Chest
- **Publisher**: Elsevier / American College of Chest Physicians (ACCP)
- **ISSN**: 0012-3692 (print), 1931-3543 (online)
- **Frequency**: Monthly (12 issues/year)
- **Impact Factor**: ~9 (JCR 2023), top-tier in pulmonary and critical care
- **Open Access**: Hybrid (optional OA with APC)
- **Acceptance rate**: ~15%
- **Peer review**: Single-blind; dedicated biostatistical review for accepted manuscripts

## Abstract Requirements

**Structured abstract, 250 words maximum:**

```
Background: [Context and rationale — knowledge gap, clinical importance]
Research Question: [Specific question or hypothesis — 1 sentence]
Study Design and Methods: [Design, setting, participants, interventions/exposures,
    primary outcome, statistical approach]
Results: [Key findings with numbers, effect sizes, CIs, P values; participant flow]
Interpretation: [Main conclusion; clinical implications; limitations caveat]
```

**Critical**: CHEST uses "Research Question" (not "Objective") and "Interpretation" (not "Conclusions"). The "Research Question" must be a single interrogative sentence. The "Interpretation" section should include a brief mention of key limitations.

## Required Sections (Original Research)

1. **Introduction** — 2-3 paragraphs; establish clinical importance; identify gap; state research question
2. **Methods**
   - Ethics: IRB approval or waiver with institution name
   - Study Design and Setting: specify design (retrospective cohort, RCT, etc.)
   - Participants: inclusion/exclusion criteria, recruitment dates
   - Interventions/Exposures and Outcomes: primary and secondary
   - Statistical Analysis: pre-specified analysis plan, software, power calculation
3. **Results** — begin with participant flow/screening; baseline table; then outcomes
4. **Discussion** — begin with key findings; interpret in context of literature; mechanisms; strengths; limitations (dedicated paragraph); future directions
5. **Conclusion** — separate brief paragraph (2-3 sentences); clinical implications emphasized

## Statistical Reporting

- Report exact P values to 2-3 significant figures (P = .032); use P < .001 for very small values.
- 95% CI required for primary outcome and major comparisons.
- Effect sizes (OR, HR, RR, mean difference) always with CI — never P value alone.
- Sample size/power justification required for RCTs; recommended for other designs.
- Multivariable models: report adjusted and unadjusted estimates; specify variable selection method.
- Diagnostic accuracy: sensitivity, specificity, AUC with 95% CI; report prevalence.
- Subgroup analyses: pre-specified vs post hoc must be stated; interaction testing.
- Missing data: report amount; describe handling (complete case, multiple imputation).
- For lung function: report percent predicted with reference equations cited.
- For imaging studies: inter-reader agreement (kappa/ICC) required.
- Statistical software and version must be stated.
- CHEST encourages Bayesian approaches with appropriate justification.

## Figures

- **Maximum 6 figures/tables combined** for Original Research
- **Resolution**: 300 DPI minimum; 600 DPI for line art
- **Format**: TIFF, EPS, PDF, or high-resolution PNG
- **Color**: Free for all figures (print and online)
- **Supplementary material (e-Online)**: unlimited e-Tables, e-Figures, e-Methods; submitted as e-Appendix
- **CT/imaging figures**: window/level settings must be reported; DICOM standards
- **Flow diagrams**: CONSORT, PRISMA, or STARD as appropriate
- **Figure legends**: must be self-explanatory; abbreviations defined within legend

## Common Rejection Reasons

1. **Scope mismatch** — studies without clear relevance to pulmonary, critical care, or sleep medicine
2. **Underpowered or small single-center study** — CHEST favors adequately powered multicenter work
3. **Poorly defined research question** — the "Research Question" in abstract must be precise and testable
4. **Methodological concerns** — inadequate control for confounders, selection bias, or immortal time bias
5. **Insufficient novelty** — incremental advances over existing guidelines or well-established evidence
6. **Statistical problems** — missing CIs, inappropriate tests, multiple comparisons without correction
7. **Lung imaging/AI without clinical validation** — purely technical studies should go to radiology journals; CHEST expects clinical endpoint or practice impact

## Positioning

CHEST is appropriate when:
- Pulmonary, critical care, or sleep study with clear clinical relevance
- Lung imaging study (CT screening, AI nodule detection, COPD quantification) with clinical outcome
- Meta-analysis or systematic review of respiratory interventions or diagnostics
- Guideline-relevant research (Lung-RADS, Fleischner, ACCP guidelines)
- Large RCT or prospective cohort in COPD, asthma, ILD, PE, or critical care

Not appropriate for: basic science without translational component, purely technical imaging/AI methodology, non-respiratory disease, small case series.

---

## CHEST Special Features

- **CHEST Guidelines and Expert Panel Reports**: major venue for ACCP evidence-based guidelines
- **Lung-RADS and CT Screening**: core content area; studies using NLST, NELSON, or screening registry data
- **Critical Care**: ventilator management, sepsis, ARDS — shared scope with CCM and AJRCCM
- **Sleep Medicine**: PSG studies, OSA interventions, CPAP outcomes
